Goals:

Start DOM reading practice and breakout, Finish Website #3, Meetup #3 write-up, Resubmit JS #3 drill, L2L video, Lightning talk outline from notes.


Reflection:

Though I failed to meet most of my goals today felt pretty good. I was feeling a little bogged down, so I jumped into DOM stuff. I got a decent win there as I felt I got back up to speed with what I was learning in JSA, and that made me excited to finish up this first bucket to get to the second. I finished the write-up and notes on my lightning talk and began to white-board wire-framing it, but I ran out of energy. I did have some decent revelations in my practice yesterday:

  1. Tackle small goals first and pepper them throughout the day to help keep working momentum going. They can help forward progress by switching to one before I become too bogged down in something.
  2. Take breaks and use a Pomodoro timer! This has helped immensely as the timer acts as a challenge, just like in a AMRAP (As Many Rounds/Rep As Possible) & RFT (Rounds For Time) workout. The breaks allow me to get up and stretch and refocus. Take care of messages and get odds and ends taken care of so my work time is that much more focused. I find myself challenging me to complete a given task in X amount of cycles, trying to stay as few as possible.
  3. set up contingencies for when I don't finish enough goals. I can hold off of Beer:30 (as I usually only drink 1 tops) in order to knock out a couple more easy tasks at the end of the day. I need after school for cooking, shopping and chores, so all the Meetups this week kinda has thrown me off, but I could have easily touched 3 goals minimum in a couple Pomodoro cycles if I so chose to yesterday. It would be the most effective use of my time and will give my brain a solid break from code, allowing it to rest and reformat for the next day. I feel more and more my groove is honing into something most effective with the goals and reflection. That and the Pomodoro timer will help immensely in life.

Wins & Losses:

I really dug into the DOM and got a good foot forward there. I was falling off in practice a little bit towards lunch so I should get better of identifying that and switching tasks to keep my focus optimal and fresh. I didn't touch website #3 or resubmit my JS drill (but will after this). I did however complete my write-up for my meetup and White-Boarded and parsed my notes for my lightning talk. Like I discussed above, starting with an easy win and peppering the lighter tasks throughout the day may keep my momentum higher along with my spirits. I am looking this weekend to try to find a few practices that I can implement to speed up my learning and retention of information. I believe it is a long process, so only changing a couple to few things at a time is the best way to measure whether or not a newly adopted practice is effective or not, and it also gives a whole week of time to explore the process to get a better idea if the process works for me, because everything feels awkward and ineffective at first, but through practice and exposure I will have a better idea if the process will be beneficial to adopt. These little goals definitely help break up the day and I really should consider being better about outlining larger tasks and breaking them into smaller ones in order to better structure my day and pomodoro timer. I am feeling like I am seeing a big chunk of puzzle coming together and that excites me. I will def be using the timer to keep my workload high at a disproportionately low stress level. Tomorrow I plan on utilizing the pomodoro timer from the get-go, when I arrive at school, to complete more in the earlier hours.
